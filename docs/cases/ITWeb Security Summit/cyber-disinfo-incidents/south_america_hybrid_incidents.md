# Hybrid Cyber/FIMI Incidents in South America

This document compiles South America-focused incidents involving both a cyber component and a foreign information manipulation/interference component, using the existing bibliography files, the uploaded Shapiro dataset PDF, the uploaded EuRepoC spreadsheet, the uploaded cyber-operations CSV, and additional reputable external reporting. The threshold remains the same: a meaningful cyber-enabled element and a meaningful information-manipulation element both need to be supportable from public sources.

## Brazil

### Incident 1

## Incident Summary

**Title:** Disinformation networks and cyber-enabled manipulation during Brazil’s 2018 election  
**Date:** 2018  
**Targets:** Brazilian voters, election discourse, and trust in democratic institutions  
**Perpetrator:** Domestic and networked online actors using messaging-platform ecosystems; public reporting documented large-scale manipulated information flows rather than one attributable foreign operator  
**Motivation:** Influence voter perceptions, polarize debate, and shape the election outcome  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Mass messaging, platform exploitation, automated or semi-automated dissemination, and cyber-enabled content amplification  
- **Systems Attacked:** WhatsApp-centered information ecosystem and social-media channels  
- **Data Held:** Contact lists, messaging groups, and political content assets; not enough info on stolen institutional datasets  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; the technical element was platform-scale coordinated dissemination  
- **Estimated Impact:** Significant distortion of campaign information flows  
- **Investigators:** Reuters, election monitors, and researchers in Latin American disinformation studies  
- **Remediation:** Fact-checking, platform policy responses, and election-authority interventions  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** False claims and conspiracy narratives spread at industrial scale online  
- **Narratives:** Anti-opposition claims, electoral misinformation, and polarizing social narratives  
- **Deception:** Viral false messaging, chain forwards, and opaque amplification networks  
- **Platforms:** WhatsApp and other social-media services  
- **Reach:** National  
- **Impact:** Reduced transparency and trust in the campaign environment  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Reuters, election observers, and Brazilian fact-checkers  
- **Remediation:** Monitoring, content moderation, and court action  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effect relied on cyber-enabled messaging infrastructure and coordinated digital amplification

### Incident 2

## Incident Summary

**Title:** Disinformation networks and institutional attacks during Brazil’s 2022 election  
**Date:** 2022  
**Targets:** Brazilian voters, Lula and Bolsonaro campaigns, and confidence in the Superior Electoral Court  
**Perpetrator:** Domestic online disinformation networks; some broader regional patterns were also documented by Reuters and rights groups  
**Motivation:** Shape electoral perceptions, undermine trust in the electoral authority, and polarize society  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated digital dissemination, platform manipulation, and video-first social propagation  
- **Systems Attacked:** TikTok, Instagram, WhatsApp, and other election-information channels  
- **Data Held:** Political content and audience-targeting/distribution networks  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration  
- **Estimated Impact:** Undermined trust in the neutrality of the national electoral body and intensified campaign toxicity  
- **Investigators:** Reuters, Carter Center, Brazilian rights groups, and election observers  
- **Remediation:** Court takedown orders, platform action, and election-integrity monitoring  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Sophisticated online disinformation attacking candidates and the electoral process  
- **Narratives:** Electoral fraud allegations, candidate smears, and distrust in election management  
- **Deception:** Misleading video content, coordinated social amplification, and false claims about the voting system  
- **Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram, WhatsApp, and other major platforms  
- **Reach:** National  
- **Impact:** Further eroded confidence in election administration  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Reuters, Carter Center, and Brazilian civil-society monitors  
- **Remediation:** Accelerated removal policies and court intervention  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The campaign depended on cyber-enabled distribution infrastructures and coordinated online amplification

## Ecuador and regional targeting

### Incident 3

## Incident Summary

**Title:** Estraterra network targeting Ecuador and multiple Latin American elections  
**Date:** 2020  
**Targets:** Ecuador, Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Uruguay, and Venezuela; South American election discourse broadly  
**Perpetrator:** Estraterra, an Ecuadorian public-relations firm operating out of Canada, with political consultants and former Ecuadorian government employees, according to Facebook and DFRLab reporting  
**Motivation:** Influence political topics and election-related debate across several Latin American states  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior through pages, user accounts, and Instagram profiles  
- **Systems Attacked:** Facebook and Instagram information ecosystems  
- **Data Held:** Fake assets, persona networks, and regionally targeted political messaging  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; technical activity centered on fake and covert platform infrastructure  
- **Estimated Impact:** Cross-border manipulation of political discourse across multiple South American elections  
- **Investigators:** Facebook and DFRLab  
- **Remediation:** Takedown of 77 pages, 41 accounts, and 56 Instagram profiles  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Covert political influence activity targeting multiple national contexts  
- **Narratives:** Election-linked candidate promotion and politically aligned messaging tailored to local contexts  
- **Deception:** Inauthentic assets and covert coordination across countries  
- **Platforms:** Facebook and Instagram  
- **Reach:** Multi-country  
- **Impact:** Distorted transparency in regional electoral discourse  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** DFRLab and Facebook threat investigators  
- **Remediation:** Platform takedowns and public reporting  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effort relied directly on cyber-enabled fake-account infrastructure and covert digital coordination

## Venezuela

### Incident 4

## Incident Summary

**Title:** Venezuelan government-linked influence campaign on Twitter  
**Date:** 2019  
**Targets:** Venezuelan political discourse and regional audiences in Latin America  
**Perpetrator:** Venezuelan government-linked network documented by DFRLab and cited in the online political influence literature  
**Motivation:** Shape domestic and regional perceptions in favor of the Maduro government  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior, fake or managed accounts, and cyber-enabled narrative amplification  
- **Systems Attacked:** Twitter political-discourse ecosystem  
- **Data Held:** Coordinated account infrastructure and political messaging assets  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No public evidence of exfiltration; the technical layer was covert digital coordination  
- **Estimated Impact:** Amplified regime-aligned narratives and reduced information transparency  
- **Investigators:** DFRLab and researchers cited in the Shapiro documentation  
- **Remediation:** Exposure and platform moderation  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Government-linked influence campaign using coordinated online accounts  
- **Narratives:** Pro-government framing, opposition criticism, and political legitimacy narratives  
- **Deception:** Coordinated amplification and inauthentic account behavior  
- **Platforms:** Twitter  
- **Reach:** Domestic and regional  
- **Impact:** Distorted online political debate  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** DFRLab and related researchers  
- **Remediation:** Public reporting and moderation  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effort depended on covert cyber-enabled account infrastructure and social-platform manipulation

## Colombia / regional Spanish-language space

### Incident 5

## Incident Summary

**Title:** Regional disinformation and propaganda flows from extra-regional state media into South American information environments  
**Date:** 2021 and after  
**Targets:** Audiences in Argentina, Colombia, Uruguay, Venezuela, and the broader South American Spanish-language space  
**Perpetrator:** Russia-, China-, and allied state-media ecosystems identified in regional disinformation research  
**Motivation:** Expand geopolitical influence and shape regional narratives  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Cyber-enabled dissemination networks, coordinated inauthentic behavior, and industrial-scale digital propaganda distribution  
- **Systems Attacked:** Social-media platforms, digital news ecosystems, and messaging channels  
- **Data Held:** Content networks, amplification accounts, and message-distribution assets  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on one incident-specific exfiltration pattern  
- **Estimated Impact:** Sustained manipulation of South American political and geopolitical discourse  
- **Investigators:** Global Americans and regional disinformation researchers  
- **Remediation:** Exposure, fact-checking, and policy recommendations  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Propaganda and disinformation distributed through digital media and social channels  
- **Narratives:** Pro-Russia, pro-China, anti-U.S., and region-specific political narratives  
- **Deception:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior, cyborg accounts, and manipulated narrative amplification  
- **Platforms:** Twitter, Facebook, YouTube, TikTok, WhatsApp, and Telegram  
- **Reach:** Region-wide  
- **Impact:** Degraded trust in information integrity and influenced perceptions on international issues  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Global Americans and cited regional analysts  
- **Remediation:** Monitoring and resilience-building proposals  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence efforts rely on cyber-enabled account networks, platform manipulation, and industrial distribution architecture

## Bolivia / Chile / Argentina / Uruguay

### Incident 6

## Incident Summary

**Title:** Latin American election-focused coordinated inauthentic behavior affecting several South American countries  
**Date:** 2020  
**Targets:** Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Uruguay, Ecuador, and Venezuela  
**Perpetrator:** Estraterra-linked network, according to Facebook and DFRLab  
**Motivation:** Influence political topics and candidate perceptions across elections in South America  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior across Facebook and Instagram  
- **Systems Attacked:** Platform information ecosystems in multiple countries  
- **Data Held:** Fake pages, profiles, and targeted content assets  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No public evidence of exfiltration; core technical component was fake network infrastructure  
- **Estimated Impact:** Cross-border distortion of political debate during electoral periods  
- **Investigators:** Facebook and DFRLab  
- **Remediation:** Platform removals and public exposure  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Covert cross-border political messaging  
- **Narratives:** Candidate promotion and country-specific election talking points  
- **Deception:** Inauthentic accounts, deceptive page identities, and covert coordination  
- **Platforms:** Facebook and Instagram  
- **Reach:** Multi-country South America  
- **Impact:** Reduced transparency in election-related discourse  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Facebook threat investigators and DFRLab  
- **Remediation:** Takedowns and disclosure  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The operation relied on cyber-enabled account infrastructure spanning several South American states
