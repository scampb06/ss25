# Hybrid Cyber/FIMI Incidents in Africa

This document compiles Africa-focused incidents involving both a cyber component and a foreign information manipulation/interference component, using the existing bibliography files, the uploaded EuRepoC spreadsheet, the uploaded Shapiro online political influence dataset documentation, the uploaded cyber-operations CSV, the attached South Africa bibliography, and additional reputable external reporting. The emphasis is on cases where a cyber-enabled element and an information-manipulation element can both be documented with enough detail to satisfy the requested threshold.

## Central African Republic

### Incident 1

## Incident Summary

**Title:** Prigozhin-linked Russian influence operation targeting the Central African Republic  
**Date:** 2019-2020  
**Targets:** Central African Republic voters, political discourse, media space, and state legitimacy  
**Perpetrator:** Russian networks linked to Yevgeniy Prigozhin and Wagner-associated influence operations  
**Motivation:** Build support for Russian interests, shape perceptions of security and governance, and increase Moscow’s leverage in CAR  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior, fake accounts, deceptive pages, and cyber-enabled distribution infrastructure rather than a single destructive network intrusion  
- **Systems Attacked:** Social-media ecosystems, digital news distribution channels, and online public discourse  
- **Data Held:** Audience targeting data, fake personas, and content-distribution assets; not enough info on exfiltrated government datasets  
- **Availability Impact:** No major outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; the core technical element was covert account networks and cross-platform dissemination  
- **Estimated Impact:** Significant shaping of public discourse around Russia’s role, elections, and security in CAR  
- **Investigators:** Graphika, Stanford Internet Observatory, Meta/Facebook, Reuters, and researchers cited in the Shapiro dataset documentation  
- **Remediation:** Platform takedowns, public exposure, and research-based attribution  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated foreign influence operation using fake local personas and media fronts  
- **Narratives:** Pro-Russian messaging, anti-French narratives, support for Russian security actors, and electoral influence themes  
- **Deception:** Fake accounts, local “franchise” personas, pages posing as independent outlets, and manipulated engagement  
- **Platforms:** Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, websites, and long-form articles  
- **Reach:** National and regional  
- **Impact:** Distorted political discourse and amplified support for Russian geopolitical interests  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Graphika, Stanford Internet Observatory, Meta, Reuters, and Africa-focused researchers  
- **Remediation:** Platform removals and public documentation  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effort depended on cyber-enabled fake-account infrastructure and covert digital dissemination networks

## Tanzania

### Incident 8

## Incident Summary

**Title:** Coordinated harassment and fake web pages targeting Tanzanian opposition and civil society leaders  
**Date:** 2021  
**Targets:** Tanzanian opposition figures, civil-society leaders, and their digital visibility  
**Perpetrator:** Networks described in Africa Center documentation using fake accounts and adversarial reporting techniques  
**Motivation:** Suppress critical voices and manipulate the information environment in favor of incumbents  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Fake web-page creation, adversarial reporting, and cyber-enabled account harassment  
- **Systems Attacked:** Social-media accounts and platform moderation systems  
- **Data Held:** Replicated target content on fake pages and complaint infrastructure  
- **Availability Impact:** Targeted leaders’ accounts were removed or impaired on some platforms  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** The core technical effect was false copyright complaints and replicated content, not exfiltration  
- **Estimated Impact:** Silencing or intimidating civil-society and opposition voices online  
- **Investigators:** Africa Center for Strategic Studies and related researchers  
- **Remediation:** Exposure and continued monitoring  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Harassment-based information manipulation and narrative distortion  
- **Narratives:** Pro-government hashtags and anti-opposition framing  
- **Deception:** Fake web pages, fake personas, and coordinated adversarial reporting  
- **Platforms:** Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn, and linked fake websites  
- **Reach:** Targeted but politically meaningful  
- **Impact:** Reduced visibility of critics and increased intimidation  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Africa Center analysts  
- **Remediation:** Public documentation and platform vigilance  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effect relied on cyber-enabled fake sites and exploitative use of platform complaint systems
