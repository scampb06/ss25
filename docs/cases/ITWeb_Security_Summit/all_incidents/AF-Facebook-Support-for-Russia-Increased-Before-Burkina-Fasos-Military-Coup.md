## Incident Summary

**Title:** Facebook support for Russia increased before Burkina Faso's military coup  
**Date:** 2022  
**Targets:** Burkinabe public opinion and the information environment surrounding the coup  
**Continent:** Africa  
**Perpetrator:** Pro-Russian Facebook assets documented by DFRLab  
**Motivation:** Build support for Russia and shape narratives around the coup and external partnerships  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated platform manipulation and inauthentic digital amplification  
- **Systems Attacked:** Facebook information ecosystem and related online public discourse  
- **Data Held:** Fake and semi-coordinated account infrastructure  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; the technical component centered on coordinated platform use  
- **Estimated Impact:** Shifted online salience toward pro-Russian narratives before a major political rupture  
- **Investigators:** DFRLab and Africa-focused disinformation researchers  
- **Remediation:** Public exposure and platform response  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated pro-Russian narrative amplification ahead of political upheaval  
- **Narratives:** Russia as preferred partner, anti-French sentiment, and legitimacy messaging around political change  
- **Deception:** Inauthentic or coordinated Facebook activity  
- **Platforms:** Facebook  
- **Reach:** National  
- **Impact:** Increased visibility of pro-Russian narratives before the coup  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** DFRLab and researchers  
- **Remediation:** Exposure and monitoring  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence campaign relied on cyber-enabled platform manipulation and coordinated digital behavior
