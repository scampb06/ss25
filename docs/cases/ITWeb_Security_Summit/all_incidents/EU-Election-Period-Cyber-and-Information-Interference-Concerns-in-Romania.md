## Incident Summary

**Title:** Election-period cyber and information interference concerns in Romania  
**Date:** 2024  
**Targets:** Romanian democratic processes, public discourse, election information space  
**Continent:** Europe
**Perpetrator:** Not enough info for a single attributed actor; Russia-linked patterns were part of the broader EU election-threat picture  
**Motivation:** Undermine electoral trust and shape democratic discourse  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Cybersecurity risks and hostile digital activity flagged in the EU election period  
- **Systems Attacked:** Not enough info  
- **Data Held:** Not enough info  
- **Availability Impact:** Not enough info  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info  
- **Estimated Impact:** Pressure on election integrity and public trust  
- **Investigators:** European Commission services and national election-security stakeholders  
- **Remediation:** Monitoring, platform coordination, stress-testing and preparedness measures  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** FIMI patterns affecting election integrity in the wider EU environment relevant to Romania  
- **Narratives:** Fraud claims, anti-EU themes, divisive political narratives  
- **Deception:** Coordinated online manipulation and inauthentic content  
- **Platforms:** Social platforms and websites  
- **Reach:** National and EU-wide information space  
- **Impact:** Lower trust in democratic processes  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** EU institutions and national authorities  
- **Remediation:** Monitoring, fact-checking, platform engagement  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The election-threat model combined cybersecurity risks with information interference
