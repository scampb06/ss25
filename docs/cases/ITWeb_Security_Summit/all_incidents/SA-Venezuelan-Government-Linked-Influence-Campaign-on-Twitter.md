## Incident Summary

**Title:** Venezuelan government-linked influence campaign on Twitter  
**Date:** 2019  
**Targets:** Venezuelan political discourse and regional audiences in Latin America  
**Continent:** South America  
**Perpetrator:** Venezuelan government-linked network documented by DFRLab and cited in the online political influence literature  
**Motivation:** Shape domestic and regional perceptions in favor of the Maduro government  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior, fake or managed accounts, and cyber-enabled narrative amplification  
- **Systems Attacked:** Twitter political-discourse ecosystem  
- **Data Held:** Coordinated account infrastructure and political messaging assets  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No public evidence of exfiltration; the technical layer was covert digital coordination  
- **Estimated Impact:** Amplified regime-aligned narratives and reduced information transparency  
- **Investigators:** DFRLab and researchers cited in the Shapiro documentation  
- **Remediation:** Exposure and platform moderation  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Government-linked influence campaign using coordinated online accounts  
- **Narratives:** Pro-government framing, opposition criticism, and political legitimacy narratives  
- **Deception:** Coordinated amplification and inauthentic account behavior  
- **Platforms:** Twitter  
- **Reach:** Domestic and regional  
- **Impact:** Distorted online political debate  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** DFRLab and related researchers  
- **Remediation:** Public reporting and moderation  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effort depended on covert cyber-enabled account infrastructure and social-platform manipulation
