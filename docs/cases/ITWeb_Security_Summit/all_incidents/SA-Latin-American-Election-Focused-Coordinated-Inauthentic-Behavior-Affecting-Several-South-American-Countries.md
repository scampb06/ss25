## Incident Summary

**Title:** Latin American election-focused coordinated inauthentic behavior affecting several South American countries  
**Date:** 2020  
**Targets:** Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Uruguay, Ecuador, and Venezuela  
**Continent:** South America  
**Perpetrator:** Estraterra-linked network, according to Facebook and DFRLab  
**Motivation:** Influence political topics and candidate perceptions across elections in South America  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior across Facebook and Instagram  
- **Systems Attacked:** Platform information ecosystems in multiple countries  
- **Data Held:** Fake pages, profiles, and targeted content assets  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No public evidence of exfiltration; core technical component was fake network infrastructure  
- **Estimated Impact:** Cross-border distortion of political debate during electoral periods  
- **Investigators:** Facebook and DFRLab  
- **Remediation:** Platform removals and public exposure  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Covert cross-border political messaging  
- **Narratives:** Candidate promotion and country-specific election talking points  
- **Deception:** Inauthentic accounts, deceptive page identities, and covert coordination  
- **Platforms:** Facebook and Instagram  
- **Reach:** Multi-country South America  
- **Impact:** Reduced transparency in election-related discourse  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Facebook threat investigators and DFRLab  
- **Remediation:** Takedowns and disclosure  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The operation relied on cyber-enabled account infrastructure spanning several South American states
