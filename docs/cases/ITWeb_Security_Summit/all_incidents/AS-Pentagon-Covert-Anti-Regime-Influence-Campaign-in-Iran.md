## Incident Summary

**Title:** Pentagon Covert Anti-Regime Influence Campaign in Iran  
**Date:** 2017-2022  
**Targets:** Iranian domestic audiences, politically engaged Persian-language social-media communities, and online audiences critical of the Islamic Republic  
**Continent:** Asia  
**Perpetrator:** Covert pro-Western influence network linked by researchers to U.S.-origin activity, including sham media and accounts amplifying U.S. government and military-linked content  
**Motivation:** Undermine trust in the Iranian regime, highlight repression and corruption, promote U.S.-aligned narratives, and counter Iranian regional influence

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior using fake personas, sham media outlets such as Dariche News, cross-platform posting, and reuse of U.S.-linked reward and military messaging
- **Systems Attacked:** Persian-language social media ecosystems on Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, Telegram, and linked websites serving Iranian audiences
- **Data Held:** Social media and website content, sham media article archives, graphics, reward notices, translated messaging, and linked amplification channels
- **Availability Impact:** No major service outages reported; the operation focused on narrative insertion and sustained visibility rather than technical disruption
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No confirmed data exfiltration; the technical component centered on covert asset operation, article dissemination, and persona-based amplification
- **Estimated Impact:** The campaign reached politically engaged Iranian clusters online and persisted for years, though most engagement metrics remained modest
- **Investigators:** Stanford Internet Observatory, Graphika, Twitter, and Meta
- **Remediation:** Platform removal of suspended accounts and pages; public attribution remained limited but the assets were exposed in open-source reporting

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Covert narrative shaping via sham outlets and persona networks emphasizing human rights, corruption, regime hypocrisy, and the IRGC’s role in repression and foreign interventions
- **Narratives:** The Iranian regime starves its people while funding Hezbollah and Hamas; the IRGC drives corruption and poverty; women’s rights are crushed in Iran; activists and minorities are imprisoned or executed; Iranian officials interfere abroad and in U.S. elections
- **Deception:** Fake personas, covert outlet branding, selective quoting of U.S. military and State Department material, and presentation of state-linked messaging as independent Persian-language commentary
- **Platforms:** Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, Telegram, and associated websites including Dariche News
- **Reach:** Modest but persistent reach into Iranian political discourse clusters over a multiyear period
- **Impact:** Sought to deepen public dissatisfaction with the Iranian regime and reinforce U.S.-aligned framing of Iranian domestic and regional politics
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Stanford Internet Observatory and Graphika
- **Remediation:** Platform takedowns, open-source scrutiny, and subsequent Pentagon review of clandestine online influence practices
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence campaign relied on covert digital infrastructure, false identities, and sham media channels to move regime-critical narratives into Iranian information spaces without transparent attribution
