## Incident Summary

**Title:** Coordinated online propaganda and fake-news activity during Zimbabwe's 2018 election period  
**Date:** 2018  
**Targets:** Zimbabwean voters, opposition narratives, and the election information environment  
**Continent:** Africa  
**Perpetrator:** Online propaganda networks and fake-news mercenaries described in reporting and cited dataset references  
**Motivation:** Shape election perceptions and discredit opponents  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Fake-account operations and cyber-enabled propaganda dissemination  
- **Systems Attacked:** Social-media ecosystems and digital political discourse  
- **Data Held:** Fake accounts and propaganda assets  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; the main technical element was coordinated online amplification  
- **Estimated Impact:** Increased confusion and polarization in the election period  
- **Investigators:** BuzzFeed News, local researchers, and references preserved in the Shapiro dataset bibliography  
- **Remediation:** Public exposure  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Fake-news and propaganda effort around the election  
- **Narratives:** Anti-opposition claims and pro-establishment framing  
- **Deception:** Fake-news mercenaries and coordinated online propaganda  
- **Platforms:** Social media and online news channels  
- **Reach:** National  
- **Impact:** Distorted campaign information environment  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Journalists and researchers  
- **Remediation:** Exposure and fact-checking  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The manipulation relied on covert cyber-enabled dissemination networks and fake online identities
