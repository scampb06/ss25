## Incident Summary

**Title:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior and online incitement around the Tigray conflict  
**Date:** 2021  
**Targets:** Ethiopian information space, conflict narratives, diaspora audiences, and ethnic-community relations  
**Continent:** Africa  
**Perpetrator:** Inauthentic networks linked in public reporting to actors supportive of the Ethiopian government and related influence communities  
**Motivation:** Shape wartime narratives, delegitimize opponents, and mobilize support  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior, click-to-tweet campaigns, and cyber-enabled social amplification  
- **Systems Attacked:** Social-media discourse and digital information ecosystems  
- **Data Held:** Messaging templates, amplification networks, and social accounts  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; digital manipulation infrastructure is the key technical component  
- **Estimated Impact:** Heightened polarization and amplification of conflict-linked messaging  
- **Investigators:** DFRLab, Meta, and researchers cited in the Shapiro dataset  
- **Remediation:** Account removals and conflict-monitoring responses  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated online messaging and inflammatory content amplification  
- **Narratives:** Pro-government narratives, anti-Tigray messaging, and ethnic mobilization  
- **Deception:** Inauthentic assets, coordinated posting, and organized amplification  
- **Platforms:** Twitter, Facebook, and other social platforms  
- **Reach:** Domestic and diaspora  
- **Impact:** Escalated online hostility and distorted the conflict information environment  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** DFRLab, Meta, and conflict analysts  
- **Remediation:** Platform action and public exposure  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effort relied on cyber-enabled social-account coordination and manipulation infrastructure
