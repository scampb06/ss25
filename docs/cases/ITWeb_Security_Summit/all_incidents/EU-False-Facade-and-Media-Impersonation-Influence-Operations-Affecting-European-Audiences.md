## Incident Summary

**Title:** False Façade and media-impersonation influence operations affecting European audiences  
**Date:** 2024  
**Targets:** European media audiences, trusted news brands, democratic information environments  
**Continent:** Europe
**Perpetrator:** Russia-linked FIMI ecosystems in EEAS reporting  
**Motivation:** Launder pro-Kremlin narratives through trusted-seeming channels  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Cyber-enabled impersonation and creation of inauthentic news sites  
- **Systems Attacked:** Public trust in media brands and online domain infrastructure  
- **Data Held:** Web infrastructure and branded site identity elements  
- **Availability Impact:** No major outage necessary  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration  
- **Estimated Impact:** Large-scale narrative laundering and audience deception  
- **Investigators:** EEAS and allied researchers  
- **Remediation:** Exposure, platform action, domain disruption  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Inauthentic news websites and impersonation of legitimate media  
- **Narratives:** Pro-Kremlin geopolitical claims and anti-Western messaging  
- **Deception:** Impersonation and credibility theft from known media brands  
- **Platforms:** Websites, X, other social platforms  
- **Reach:** Wide multi-country reach  
- **Impact:** Audience deception and weakened confidence in legitimate media  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** EEAS and partner researchers  
- **Remediation:** Public exposure and removal actions  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence operation was enabled by technical creation and management of deceptive web infrastructure
