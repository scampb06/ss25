## Incident Summary

**Title:** Polish election-related cyber pressure and anti-Ukraine information manipulation  
**Date:** 2023-2024  
**Targets:** Polish democratic discourse, public institutions, support for Ukraine, Polish public opinion  
**Continent:** Europe
**Perpetrator:** Russia-linked actors according to EEAS and wider reporting  
**Motivation:** Weaken support for Ukraine, polarize domestic politics, undermine democratic trust  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Cyber pressure and hostile online infrastructure activity in the electoral environment  
- **Systems Attacked:** Public-sector and political information infrastructure; exact systems not enough info  
- **Data Held:** Not enough info  
- **Availability Impact:** Not enough info  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info  
- **Estimated Impact:** Hybrid pressure on election integrity and public confidence  
- **Investigators:** EEAS, national authorities, open-source researchers  
- **Remediation:** Monitoring, platform coordination, election preparedness  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated anti-Ukraine and anti-EU FIMI targeting Poland  
- **Narratives:** Burden-sharing resentment, anti-refugee narratives, anti-EU and anti-Ukraine messaging  
- **Deception:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior and manipulative framing  
- **Platforms:** X, websites, social media networks  
- **Reach:** National and regional  
- **Impact:** Social division and reduced trust in policy choices  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** EEAS and civil-society researchers  
- **Remediation:** Exposure, fact-checking, awareness campaigns  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The operation depended on cyber-enabled dissemination channels and digital manipulation
