## Incident Summary

**Title:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior and electoral disinformation concerns around Australian democratic events  
**Date:** 2023-2025  
**Targets:** Australian elections and referendum-related public discourse  
**Continent:** Australia  
**Perpetrator:** Mixed and not always publicly attributable; platform transparency reporting and third-party research identified fake profiles and coordinated manipulation  
**Motivation:** Influence voter opinion and shape debate around democratic events  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Fake profiles, coordinated inauthentic behavior, and platform manipulation  
- **Systems Attacked:** Facebook, Instagram, X, and related online debate spaces  
- **Data Held:** Fake-account networks and political narrative content  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No specific exfiltration case required; the technical component was covert digital account infrastructure  
- **Estimated Impact:** Raised recurring concern over the vulnerability of Australian democratic events to cyber-enabled manipulation  
- **Investigators:** Meta transparency reporting, Australian code-of-practice processes, and external researchers  
- **Remediation:** Content action, CIB disruption, and election-integrity measures  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Election-related misinformation and coordinated online manipulation  
- **Narratives:** Vary by event; generally aimed at shaping political preferences and trust in institutions  
- **Deception:** Fake profiles and covertly coordinated posting behavior  
- **Platforms:** Meta platforms and X  
- **Reach:** National  
- **Impact:** Contributed to institutional concern about foreign interference and democratic resilience  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Meta and Australian digital-policy stakeholders  
- **Remediation:** Transparency reports, moderation, and election safeguards  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The campaigns operated through cyber-enabled fake accounts and coordinated digital infrastructure
