## Incident Summary

**Title:** Pro-Russian Facebook assets supporting Wagner-linked narratives and anti-democracy protests in Mali  
**Date:** 2022  
**Targets:** Malian public opinion, anti-democracy protest dynamics, perceptions of Russia and Wagner  
**Continent:** Africa  
**Perpetrator:** Pro-Russian influence networks documented by DFRLab and Africa Center materials  
**Motivation:** Increase local support for Russia and Wagner, delegitimize Western partners, and shape the post-coup political environment  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior and cyber-enabled social-media manipulation  
- **Systems Attacked:** Facebook-centered digital public sphere and related information ecosystems  
- **Data Held:** Fake assets, engagement networks, and content-distribution infrastructure  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; the technical layer centered on fake and coordinated social-media assets  
- **Estimated Impact:** Increased narrative support for Russian involvement and protest mobilization  
- **Investigators:** DFRLab, Africa Center, and regional analysts  
- **Remediation:** Platform takedowns, public reporting, and exposure  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated pro-Russian and anti-democracy propaganda  
- **Narratives:** Support for Wagner, anti-Western narratives, anti-democracy protest framing, and pro-Russian security messaging  
- **Deception:** Inauthentic Facebook assets and covert amplification  
- **Platforms:** Facebook and adjacent online spaces  
- **Reach:** National  
- **Impact:** Reinforced pro-Russian sentiment and political polarization  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** DFRLab and Africa Center analysts  
- **Remediation:** Exposure and account removal  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The operation depended on covert cyber-enabled account networks and platform manipulation
