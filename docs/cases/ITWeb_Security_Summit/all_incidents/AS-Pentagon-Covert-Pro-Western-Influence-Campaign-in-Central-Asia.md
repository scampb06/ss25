## Incident Summary

**Title:** Pentagon Covert Pro-Western Influence Campaign in Central Asia  
**Date:** 2020-2022 (with broader campaign family active from 2017-2022)  
**Targets:** Russian-speaking publics in Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, and Tajikistan; Central Asian social-media communities and regional information spaces  
**Continent:** Asia  
**Perpetrator:** Covert pro-Western influence network linked by researchers to U.S.-origin activity and overlapping with U.S. military-linked media ecosystems  
**Motivation:** Promote U.S. and allied interests in Central Asia, criticize Russian foreign policy, highlight Chinese abuses, and weaken support for Russian narratives after the invasion of Ukraine

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior across Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, Telegram, YouTube, VKontakte, and Odnoklassniki using fake personas, sham media outlets, likely purchased followers, and synchronized posting patterns
- **Systems Attacked:** Social media platforms and online information communities serving Central Asian audiences; sham media websites including Intergazeta and associated outlet infrastructure
- **Data Held:** Social media account credentials, sham outlet content, translated articles and embassy material, engagement data, and audience-building assets such as bought followers
- **Availability Impact:** No major outages reported; the operation sought persistent insertion into information channels rather than disruption of service
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No confirmed data exfiltration; the technical emphasis was false personas, coordinated posting, translated/copied content, and artificial audience inflation
- **Estimated Impact:** Limited organic traction overall, but the network operated across multiple platforms and produced some high-view Facebook videos while embedding itself in Russian-speaking Central Asian groups
- **Investigators:** Stanford Internet Observatory, Graphika, Twitter, and Meta
- **Remediation:** Platform takedowns on Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram; continued researcher exposure of active remnants on Telegram, VK, and Odnoklassniki

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Sham independent media and persona-driven narrative shaping focused on geopolitics, aid, and regional loyalties
- **Narratives:** Praise for American aid to Central Asia; criticism of Russia and its war in Ukraine; support for cooperation among Central Asian states; attention to Uighur repression and Chinese influence; pressure for Kazakhstan to limit Russian media influence
- **Deception:** Fake personas, fake media brands, doctored or stolen profile photos, copied and lightly modified articles, Russian translations of English-language source material, and coordinated cross-platform reposting
- **Platforms:** Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, Telegram, YouTube, VKontakte, Odnoklassniki, and associated websites
- **Reach:** Generally weak engagement, though some videos went viral on Facebook and the operation infiltrated regional social groups
- **Impact:** Attempted to shape Central Asian public opinion in favor of U.S.-aligned narratives and against Russian influence, while masking the real origin of the messaging
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Stanford Internet Observatory and Graphika
- **Remediation:** Platform enforcement, public reporting, and archival analysis of sham outlets and cross-platform behavior
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effort depended on technical creation and management of sham websites, fake accounts, coordinated posting systems, and platform-native manipulation tactics to distribute the narratives
