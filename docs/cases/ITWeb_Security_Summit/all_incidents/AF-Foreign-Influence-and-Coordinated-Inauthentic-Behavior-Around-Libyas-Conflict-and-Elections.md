## Incident Summary

**Title:** Foreign influence and coordinated inauthentic behavior around Libya's conflict and elections  
**Date:** 2019-2020  
**Targets:** Libyan voters, conflict narratives, electoral politics, and public opinion  
**Continent:** Africa  
**Perpetrator:** Russian-linked and other foreign-backed influence actors documented by Stanford/Graphika and the Shapiro dataset references  
**Motivation:** Shape perceptions of conflict legitimacy, candidate standing, and foreign security partnerships  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior, fake accounts, fake media assets, and cyber-enabled cross-platform dissemination  
- **Systems Attacked:** Social-media platforms and digital media ecosystems  
- **Data Held:** Persona networks, pages, and content assets; not enough info on stolen institutional data  
- **Availability Impact:** No major outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; the technical core was networked inauthentic online behavior  
- **Estimated Impact:** Increased confusion, foreign influence penetration, and narrative distortion  
- **Investigators:** Stanford Internet Observatory, Graphika, DFRLab, and sources referenced in the Shapiro dataset  
- **Remediation:** Platform takedowns and research exposure  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated social-media manipulation and pseudo-media activity  
- **Narratives:** Pro-foreign intervention, security narratives, anti-rival faction messaging, and electoral framing  
- **Deception:** Fake local personas, false media authenticity, and cross-platform amplification  
- **Platforms:** Facebook, Twitter, websites, and social amplification networks  
- **Reach:** National and regional  
- **Impact:** Distorted understanding of the conflict and electoral process  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Stanford Internet Observatory, Graphika, DFRLab, and journalists  
- **Remediation:** Account takedowns and reporting  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The operation relied on covert cyber-enabled distribution infrastructure and inauthentic account networks
