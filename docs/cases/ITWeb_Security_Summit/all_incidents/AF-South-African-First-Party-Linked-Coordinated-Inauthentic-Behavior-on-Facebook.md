## Incident Summary

**Title:** South African First party-linked coordinated inauthentic behavior on Facebook  
**Date:** 2020  
**Targets:** South African public opinion and online political discourse  
**Continent:** Africa  
**Perpetrator:** Network linked in Africa Center documentation to South African First-related activity  
**Motivation:** Influence public opinion and promote politically aligned narratives  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior using fake and misleading pages, including pseudo-fact-checking and leak-style pages  
- **Systems Attacked:** Facebook public-discourse ecosystem  
- **Data Held:** Inauthentic pages, groups, and content-distribution assets  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No public evidence of data theft; the technical component was platform manipulation using fake pages  
- **Estimated Impact:** Distorted South African political discussion online  
- **Investigators:** Africa Center for Strategic Studies and platform investigators  
- **Remediation:** Documentation and takedown actions where applicable  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic political messaging  
- **Narratives:** Politically aligned themes promoted through fake "fact-checking" and "leak" pages  
- **Deception:** Fake and misleading page identities and authenticity laundering  
- **Platforms:** Facebook  
- **Reach:** National  
- **Impact:** Misled users and degraded trust in online political content  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Africa Center and platform investigators  
- **Remediation:** Exposure and moderation  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The operation used cyber-enabled fake-page infrastructure to manipulate the information environment
