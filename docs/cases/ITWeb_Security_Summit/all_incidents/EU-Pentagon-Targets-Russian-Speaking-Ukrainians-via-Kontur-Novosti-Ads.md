## Incident Summary

**Title:** Pentagon Targets Russian-Speaking Ukrainians via Kontur Novosti Ads  
**Date:** 2023-2026  
**Targets:** Russian-speaking audiences in Ukraine, especially in Dnipropetrovsk, Odessa, Kyiv, and Kharkiv oblasts; broader Russian-language audiences exposed to anti-Kremlin and pro-Ukraine content  
**Continent:** Europe  
**Perpetrator:** Pentagon-linked gc_ network with public advertising links to General Dynamics Information Technology and infrastructure links to prior CENTCOM-linked media ecosystems  
**Motivation:** Undermine Kremlin narratives, bolster pro-Ukraine messaging among Russian-speaking audiences, and influence wartime perceptions near the front lines

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Shared-code pseudo-news outlet supported by targeted Meta advertising and linked tracking infrastructure
- **Systems Attacked:** Meta ad systems, Russian-language audience segments in Ukrainian oblasts, and associated web-distribution channels
- **Data Held:** Russian-language site content, ad-targeting settings, gc_ site metadata, and cross-network technical markers shared with other Pentagon-linked outlets
- **Availability Impact:** No outage reported; the operation used audience segmentation and platform advertising rather than service disruption
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No confirmed exfiltration; the technical element consisted of targeted ad delivery and semi-covert site infrastructure
- **Estimated Impact:** Specific aggregate audience figures for Kontur are not public in the article, but the site was among the gc_ outlets promoted through Meta and tied to the larger high-view gc_ ecosystem
- **Investigators:** Renée DiResta and associated reporting drawing on ad-transparency data and infrastructure analysis
- **Remediation:** Public exposure of the network and closer scrutiny of disclaimer compliance for state-linked ads

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** News-style influence content aimed at wartime opinion shaping among Russian-speaking users in and around Ukraine
- **Narratives:** Anti-Kremlin themes, pro-Ukraine war framing, and content meant to influence Russian-speaking Ukrainians near the front lines
- **Deception:** Generic or weak disclosure of U.S. funding instead of clear operational attribution, plus pseudo-independent media presentation
- **Platforms:** Website, Meta platforms, and likely X-linked distribution within the wider gc_ network
- **Reach:** Targeted rather than mass-public in the available reporting, focused on specific Ukrainian oblasts
- **Impact:** Sought to shape perceptions in a live conflict environment among audiences whose language profile made them susceptible to Russian-language messaging
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Renée DiResta and journalists working from public ad-transparency and infrastructure clues
- **Remediation:** Public reporting and platform review of missing disclaimers and state-linked advertising
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effort depends on ad-tech targeting, shared technical infrastructure, and pseudo-news branding to move wartime narratives into a specific language and geographic audience segment
