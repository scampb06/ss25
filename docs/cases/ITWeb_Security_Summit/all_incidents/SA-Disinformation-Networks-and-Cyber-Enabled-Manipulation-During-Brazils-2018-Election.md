## Incident Summary

**Title:** Disinformation networks and cyber-enabled manipulation during Brazil's 2018 election  
**Date:** 2018  
**Targets:** Brazilian voters, election discourse, and trust in democratic institutions  
**Continent:** South America  
**Perpetrator:** Domestic and networked online actors using messaging-platform ecosystems; public reporting documented large-scale manipulated information flows rather than one attributable foreign operator  
**Motivation:** Influence voter perceptions, polarize debate, and shape the election outcome  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Mass messaging, platform exploitation, automated or semi-automated dissemination, and cyber-enabled content amplification  
- **Systems Attacked:** WhatsApp-centered information ecosystem and social-media channels  
- **Data Held:** Contact lists, messaging groups, and political content assets; not enough info on stolen institutional datasets  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; the technical element was platform-scale coordinated dissemination  
- **Estimated Impact:** Significant distortion of campaign information flows  
- **Investigators:** Reuters, election monitors, and researchers in Latin American disinformation studies  
- **Remediation:** Fact-checking, platform policy responses, and election-authority interventions  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** False claims and conspiracy narratives spread at industrial scale online  
- **Narratives:** Anti-opposition claims, electoral misinformation, and polarizing social narratives  
- **Deception:** Viral false messaging, chain forwards, and opaque amplification networks  
- **Platforms:** WhatsApp and other social-media services  
- **Reach:** National  
- **Impact:** Reduced transparency and trust in the campaign environment  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Reuters, election observers, and Brazilian fact-checkers  
- **Remediation:** Monitoring, content moderation, and court action  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effect relied on cyber-enabled messaging infrastructure and coordinated digital amplification
