## Incident Summary

**Title:** Cyber intrusion into Australian political parties and broader concerns about foreign election interference  
**Date:** 2019  
**Targets:** Australia's major political parties, parliamentary networks, and election-related information environment  
**Continent:** Australia  
**Perpetrator:** Public attribution focused on a sophisticated state actor; Australia did not publicly name the actor at the time, while public commentary often pointed to China  
**Motivation:** Gain political intelligence and potentially support foreign interference in Australia's democratic processes  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Intrusion into political-party computer networks and parliamentary systems  
- **Systems Attacked:** Networks of the Liberal, Labor, and National parties, as well as parliamentary systems  
- **Data Held:** Political communications, internal documents, and potentially sensitive strategic information  
- **Availability Impact:** No major public outage reported  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Officials said the intrusion was discovered before major damage was identified, but intelligence collection risk was serious  
- **Estimated Impact:** Raised concern about democratic vulnerability and the use of cyber espionage against political parties  
- **Investigators:** Australian government agencies, parliamentary security officials, and public analysts  
- **Remediation:** Incident response, network hardening, and national warnings about election interference  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** The case was publicly discussed as part of a wider pattern of cyber-enabled foreign interference around elections rather than a proven hack-and-leak campaign  
- **Narratives:** Concerns focused on sovereignty, electoral integrity, and foreign manipulation of democratic processes  
- **Deception:** Covert access to political systems by a foreign actor created the enabling condition for possible influence or interference  
- **Platforms:** Political networks and the broader information environment  
- **Reach:** National  
- **Impact:** Increased distrust and policy urgency around election security and foreign interference  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Australian officials, parliamentary committees, and policy researchers  
- **Remediation:** Public awareness and stronger democratic-resilience planning  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The cyber intrusion was treated by Australian institutions as part of the same risk environment as foreign information interference
