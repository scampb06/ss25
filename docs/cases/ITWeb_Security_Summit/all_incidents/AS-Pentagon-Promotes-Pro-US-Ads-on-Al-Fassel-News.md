## Incident Summary

**Title:** Pentagon Promotes Pro-US Ads on Al-Fassel News  
**Date:** 2023-2026  
**Targets:** Arabic-speaking audiences in Lebanon, Yemen, and Egypt; broader Middle Eastern audiences exposed to narratives about Iran, the Houthis, Hamas, Gaza, and Gulf security  
**Continent:** Asia  
**Perpetrator:** Pentagon-linked third-generation gc_ network, with public ad-buy traces tied to General Dynamics Information Technology and infrastructure links to prior CENTCOM-linked media ecosystems  
**Motivation:** Shape regional public opinion in favor of U.S. and Gulf security narratives and against Iranian and proxy actors while reducing overt U.S. military attribution

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Semi-overt propaganda distribution through a shared technical codebase, common tracking infrastructure, platform advertising, and coordinated outlet-branded social accounts
- **Systems Attacked:** X, Meta ad systems, Arabic-language social-media and web audiences, and the surrounding discovery and engagement infrastructure
- **Data Held:** Arabic-language site content, ad creative, audience-targeting parameters, internal gc_ version tags, and tracking metadata linked to shared Google Tag Manager infrastructure
- **Availability Impact:** No service outage reported; the operation focused on buying reach and inserting narratives into users’ feeds
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No confirmed data exfiltration; the technical layer consisted of advertising, tracking, and semi-covert media infrastructure rather than intrusion
- **Estimated Impact:** Part of a broader gc_ network whose X posts exceeded a combined 67 million views, with Al-Fassel specifically running country-segmented campaigns and some ads flagged by Meta for lacking required disclaimers
- **Investigators:** Renée DiResta, Sam Biddle, and public ad-transparency records reviewed in reporting
- **Remediation:** Public exposure of the network, scrutiny of disclosure language, and platform enforcement where ads lacked required disclaimers

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** State-linked news-style influence content presented with attenuated provenance rather than overt military branding
- **Narratives:** Anti-Iran, anti-Houthi, anti-Hamas, and pro-U.S./Gulf regional security framing; selective presentation of events in Gaza and the wider Middle East to reinforce U.S. strategic narratives
- **Deception:** Generic U.S.-government funding language instead of explicit CENTCOM branding, no clear disclosure on social profiles, and advertising-driven presentation that makes state-funded messaging look like ordinary news content
- **Platforms:** Website, X, Meta platforms, and Instagram-linked content distribution
- **Reach:** Significant paid reach as part of a broader ad-driven network, though direct engagement often came from real users rather than bot farms
- **Impact:** Increased exposure of targeted Arabic-speaking audiences to Pentagon-linked strategic narratives under weakly disclosed branding
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Renée DiResta and journalists including Sam Biddle
- **Remediation:** Public reporting, archive and infrastructure analysis, and closer review of ad-disclosure compliance
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The campaign’s influence effect depends on technical ad-targeting, shared tracking infrastructure, and semi-covert website architecture rather than on overt government broadcasting alone
