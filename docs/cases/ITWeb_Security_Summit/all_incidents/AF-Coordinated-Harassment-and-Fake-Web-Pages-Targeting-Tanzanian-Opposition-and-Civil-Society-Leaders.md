## Incident Summary

**Title:** Coordinated harassment and fake web pages targeting Tanzanian opposition and civil society leaders  
**Date:** 2021  
**Targets:** Tanzanian opposition figures, civil-society leaders, and their digital visibility  
**Continent:** Africa  
**Perpetrator:** Networks described in Africa Center documentation using fake accounts and adversarial reporting techniques  
**Motivation:** Suppress critical voices and manipulate the information environment in favor of incumbents  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Fake web-page creation, adversarial reporting, and cyber-enabled account harassment  
- **Systems Attacked:** Social-media accounts and platform moderation systems  
- **Data Held:** Replicated target content on fake pages and complaint infrastructure  
- **Availability Impact:** Targeted leaders' accounts were removed or impaired on some platforms  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** The core technical effect was false copyright complaints and replicated content, not exfiltration  
- **Estimated Impact:** Silencing or intimidating civil-society and opposition voices online  
- **Investigators:** Africa Center for Strategic Studies and related researchers  
- **Remediation:** Exposure and continued monitoring  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Harassment-based information manipulation and narrative distortion  
- **Narratives:** Pro-government hashtags and anti-opposition framing  
- **Deception:** Fake web pages, fake personas, and coordinated adversarial reporting  
- **Platforms:** Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn, and linked fake websites  
- **Reach:** Targeted but politically meaningful  
- **Impact:** Reduced visibility of critics and increased intimidation  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Africa Center analysts  
- **Remediation:** Public documentation and platform vigilance  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effect relied on cyber-enabled fake sites and exploitative use of platform complaint systems
