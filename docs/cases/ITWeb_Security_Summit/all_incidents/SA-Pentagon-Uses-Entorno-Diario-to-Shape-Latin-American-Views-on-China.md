## Incident Summary

**Title:** Pentagon Uses Entorno Diario to Shape Latin American Views on China  
**Date:** July 2023-September 2025  
**Targets:** Latin American audiences, especially Spanish- and Portuguese-speaking users exposed to coverage of China’s role in regional infrastructure, mining, surveillance, organized crime, narcotrafficking, and security; countries discussed include Colombia, Venezuela, Bolivia, and others in the region  
**Continent:** South America  
**Perpetrator:** Pentagon-linked gc_ network, with ad-buy traces tied to General Dynamics Information Technology and technical links to earlier CENTCOM-linked media infrastructure  
**Motivation:** Portray China as exploitative, dangerous, and strategically corrosive in Latin America while presenting the content through a pseudo-independent regional news brand

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Pseudo-news website with shared gc_ codebase, paid promotion on X and other platforms, and weakly attributed state-funded media infrastructure
- **Systems Attacked:** X distribution systems, Latin American social-media feeds, web discovery channels, and online audiences in Spanish, English, and Portuguese
- **Data Held:** Hundreds of archived site articles, ad-promoted posts, byline and content-management data, audience metrics, and shared technical markers linking Entorno Diario to the wider gc_ network
- **Availability Impact:** No outage reported; the operation aimed to saturate feeds and shape perceptions through paid visibility
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No confirmed data exfiltration; the technical mechanism centered on ad-driven amplification, pseudo-news branding, and opaque outlet provenance
- **Estimated Impact:** Entorno Diario alone generated more than 56 million views across 227 X posts, with archived analysis recovering 553 URLs published between July 2023 and September 2025
- **Investigators:** Renée DiResta and associated technical analysis using archives and ad-transparency records
- **Remediation:** Public exposure of the site, archive preservation after the site went offline, and closer scrutiny of state-linked ad buys and disclosure standards

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Agenda-driven influence content that used mostly factual stories but framed them to imply Chinese corruption, exploitation, surveillance, or criminality across Latin America
- **Narratives:** China exploits Latin American infrastructure and mining; Chinese entities enable surveillance and organized crime; Chinese regional engagement threatens sovereignty and public safety; local crises are read through a China-risk lens
- **Deception:** Pseudo-independent news branding, weak disclosure of U.S. government funding, suggestive headline framing, causal overreach, and uncertain or pseudonymous bylines across the gc_ site family
- **Platforms:** Website, X, and wider ad-driven social-media distribution
- **Reach:** Very high for a small-branded outlet, with repeated six- and seven-figure view counts on promoted Spanish-language posts
- **Impact:** Shaped or attempted to shape regional perceptions of China through sustained, state-linked editorial framing that appeared to be ordinary journalism
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Renée DiResta and public users who challenged the content using Grok and Community Notes
- **Remediation:** Public reporting, community scrutiny, and preservation of archives after takedown of the live site
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence campaign relied on technical ad-delivery systems, shared infrastructure, and weak provenance to inject anti-China narratives into Latin American information spaces at scale
