## Incident Summary

**Title:** Canada federal election AI-enabled cyber and disinformation threat environment  
**Date:** 2025  
**Targets:** Canadian federal election, Canadian voters, political parties, diaspora communities  
**Continent:** North America  
**Perpetrator:** Expected activity from PRC-, Russia-, and Iran-linked cyber and influence actors according to CSE assessments  
**Motivation:** Sow division, push foreign-state narratives, and interfere with democratic discourse  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Anticipated phishing, hack-and-leak attempts, cyber-enabled political targeting, and AI-supported interference methods  
- **Systems Attacked:** Political-party systems, politician accounts, digital campaign infrastructure  
- **Data Held:** Politically relevant voter and party data, communications, and campaign materials  
- **Availability Impact:** Not enough info on specific outages because the assessment is preemptive rather than incident-specific  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Hack-and-leak activity assessed as likely; no single confirmed exfiltration incident detailed in the cited sources  
- **Estimated Impact:** Potential voter manipulation, reputational damage, and pressure on democratic confidence  
- **Investigators:** Communications Security Establishment Canada and Canadian Centre for Cyber Security  
- **Remediation:** Threat assessments, awareness campaigns, election protections, and cyber monitoring  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** AI-enabled misinformation and disinformation targeting Canadian voters  
- **Narratives:** Divisive claims tailored to foreign-state interests, especially among targeted communities  
- **Deception:** Deepfakes, synthetic content, and manipulative online narratives  
- **Platforms:** Social media, digital campaign channels, online communities  
- **Reach:** National, including diaspora communities  
- **Impact:** Increased risk of polarization and democratic distrust  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** CSE, Canadian Centre for Cyber Security, foreign-interference inquiry structures  
- **Remediation:** Public warnings, digital literacy, and monitoring  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** Canadian authorities explicitly assessed hack-and-leak operations and disinformation as part of the same interference threat environment
