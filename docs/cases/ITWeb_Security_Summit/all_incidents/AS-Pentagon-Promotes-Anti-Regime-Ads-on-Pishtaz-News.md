## Incident Summary

**Title:** Pentagon Promotes Anti-Regime Ads on Pishtaz News  
**Date:** 2023-2026  
**Targets:** Persian-speaking audiences in Iran and Iranian information spaces concerned with economic crisis, repression, military activity, and internet shutdowns  
**Continent:** Asia  
**Perpetrator:** Pentagon-linked gc_ network with infrastructure ties to prior CENTCOM-linked outlets and ad-buy traces tied to General Dynamics Information Technology  
**Motivation:** Increase pressure on the Iranian regime by amplifying narratives about repression, economic distress, and regime vulnerability under reduced overt U.S. military attribution

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Shared-code pseudo-news site promoted via platform advertising, tracking infrastructure, and outlet-branded social distribution
- **Systems Attacked:** Persian-language online audiences on X, Meta platforms, Instagram-style content spaces, and associated web discovery systems
- **Data Held:** Farsi-language articles, ad-targeting and promotion content, gc_ version tags, and tracking metadata linked to the shared Google Tag Manager container
- **Availability Impact:** No outage reported; the operation purchased reach rather than disrupting services
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** No confirmed exfiltration; the technical element lies in covert provenance, targeted ad delivery, and coordinated site architecture
- **Estimated Impact:** Pishtaz was part of the broader gc_ network with high aggregate X visibility, and it had active Meta ads in May 2026 on internet shutdowns and economic distress in Iran
- **Investigators:** Renée DiResta, journalists, and public ad-transparency documentation
- **Remediation:** Public exposure of the network and scrutiny of platform ad labeling and disclosure practices

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** News-style influence content that mixes factual reporting with strategic topic selection and weak provenance to steer Iranian audience perceptions
- **Narratives:** Iran’s military adventurism and repression harm ordinary citizens; economic pain and internet shutdowns reveal regime fragility; the government is vulnerable and failing its population
- **Deception:** Generic U.S.-funding disclosure instead of clear military attribution, weak or absent disclosure on social profiles, and at least one case of AI-generated newscaster use in related reporting on the new site family
- **Platforms:** Website, X, Meta platforms, and Instagram-linked content distribution
- **Reach:** Meaningful paid reach as part of the third-generation gc_ network, though exact audience figures for Pishtaz alone are not fully public
- **Impact:** Sustained exposure of Iranian audiences to U.S.-aligned anti-regime framing under a pseudo-independent media identity
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Renée DiResta and journalists investigating Pentagon-linked online influence campaigns
- **Remediation:** Public scrutiny, archive and infrastructure analysis, and closer review of state-linked advertising disclosures
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effort relies on technical site infrastructure, advertising delivery, and opaque provenance to circulate regime-critical content as if it were ordinary independent media output
