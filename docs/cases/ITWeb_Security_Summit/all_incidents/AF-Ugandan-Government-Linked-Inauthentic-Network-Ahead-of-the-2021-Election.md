## Incident Summary

**Title:** Ugandan government-linked inauthentic network ahead of the 2021 election  
**Date:** 2021  
**Targets:** Ugandan election discourse, opposition figures, and public opinion  
**Continent:** Africa  
**Perpetrator:** Accounts linked by Facebook and DFRLab to Uganda's Ministry of Information and Communications Technology  
**Motivation:** Shape election narratives, attack opposition voices, and manage domestic political perceptions  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior using fake or deceptive digital accounts  
- **Systems Attacked:** Facebook ecosystem and election-related online discourse  
- **Data Held:** Fake accounts, pages, and messaging infrastructure  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; the operation centered on covert digital influence infrastructure  
- **Estimated Impact:** Distorted election discourse and manipulation of online visibility  
- **Investigators:** Meta/Facebook, DFRLab, and researchers catalogued in Shapiro  
- **Remediation:** Removal of inauthentic assets and public disclosure  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Covert domestic influence operation using digital deception  
- **Narratives:** Pro-government messaging and opposition criticism  
- **Deception:** Fake and duplicate accounts, coordinated posting, and authenticity laundering  
- **Platforms:** Facebook  
- **Reach:** National  
- **Impact:** Manipulated political debate in the election period  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Meta/Facebook and DFRLab  
- **Remediation:** Platform takedowns  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence operation depended on covert cyber-enabled account infrastructure
