## Incident Summary

**Title:** DoppelgÃ¤nger impersonation campaigns targeting Europe  
**Date:** 2022-2024  
**Targets:** European publics, media consumers, government reputations, election information environments  
**Continent:** Europe
**Perpetrator:** Pro-Russian influence operators according to EU reporting and open-source investigations  
**Motivation:** Undermine support for Ukraine, fracture European unity, distort political debate  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Cyber-enabled impersonation, domain abuse, cloned websites, infrastructure abuse  
- **Systems Attacked:** Media brands and online information ecosystems through spoofing and impersonation  
- **Data Held:** Website infrastructure, brand identity elements, content delivery channels  
- **Availability Impact:** No major outage required for the operation  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on direct exfiltration; main technical element was infrastructure abuse  
- **Estimated Impact:** Reputational and political harm across multiple European states  
- **Investigators:** EEAS, European Commission services, DFRLab, other OSINT researchers  
- **Remediation:** Domain takedowns, exposure reports, platform enforcement actions  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Fake or mirrored media websites spreading pro-Kremlin narratives in multiple languages  
- **Narratives:** Anti-Ukraine, anti-EU, anti-sanctions, anti-democratic narratives  
- **Deception:** Impersonation of legitimate media outlets and institutions  
- **Platforms:** Fake news sites, Facebook, X, other social platforms  
- **Reach:** Multi-country reach across Europe  
- **Impact:** Erosion of trust in legitimate media and democratic discourse  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** EEAS, European Commission, DFRLab, researchers  
- **Remediation:** Public exposure, technical disruption, platform takedowns  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The campaign depended on cyber-enabled cloning, spoofing, and web infrastructure abuse
