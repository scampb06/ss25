## Incident Summary

**Title:** Estraterra network targeting Ecuador and multiple Latin American elections  
**Date:** 2020  
**Targets:** Ecuador, Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Uruguay, and Venezuela; South American election discourse broadly  
**Continent:** South America  
**Perpetrator:** Estraterra, an Ecuadorian public-relations firm operating out of Canada, with political consultants and former Ecuadorian government employees, according to Facebook and DFRLab reporting  
**Motivation:** Influence political topics and election-related debate across several Latin American states  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Coordinated inauthentic behavior through pages, user accounts, and Instagram profiles  
- **Systems Attacked:** Facebook and Instagram information ecosystems  
- **Data Held:** Fake assets, persona networks, and regionally targeted political messaging  
- **Availability Impact:** No outage required  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Not enough info on exfiltration; technical activity centered on fake and covert platform infrastructure  
- **Estimated Impact:** Cross-border manipulation of political discourse across multiple South American elections  
- **Investigators:** Facebook and DFRLab  
- **Remediation:** Takedown of 77 pages, 41 accounts, and 56 Instagram profiles  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** Covert political influence activity targeting multiple national contexts  
- **Narratives:** Election-linked candidate promotion and politically aligned messaging tailored to local contexts  
- **Deception:** Inauthentic assets and covert coordination across countries  
- **Platforms:** Facebook and Instagram  
- **Reach:** Multi-country  
- **Impact:** Distorted transparency in regional electoral discourse  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** DFRLab and Facebook threat investigators  
- **Remediation:** Platform takedowns and public reporting  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The influence effort relied directly on cyber-enabled fake-account infrastructure and covert digital coordination
