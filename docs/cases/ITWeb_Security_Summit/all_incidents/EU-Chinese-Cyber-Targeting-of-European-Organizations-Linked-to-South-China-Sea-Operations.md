## Incident Summary

**Title:** Chinese cyber targeting of European organizations linked to South China Sea operations, including German and Norwegian entities  
**Date:** 2022  
**Targets:** German, Norwegian, Swedish, and other European organizations involved in wind-turbine maintenance and related industrial activity  
**Continent:** Europe
**Perpetrator:** APT40 / China-linked threat activity  
**Motivation:** Espionage and strategic influence tied to geopolitical competition over the South China Sea  

## Cyberattack Details

- **Nature:** Espionage campaign using web-based malware and scanning tools  
- **Systems Attacked:** Organizations engaged in wind-turbine production and maintenance and related industrial operations  
- **Data Held:** Industrial and operational information relevant to maritime energy infrastructure  
- **Availability Impact:** No major outage publicly reported  
- **Data Exfiltration/Alteration:** Espionage and information collection were the primary objectives  
- **Estimated Impact:** Exposure of commercially and strategically relevant information  
- **Investigators:** Proofpoint and other security researchers  
- **Remediation:** Detection, hardening, and threat advisories  

## Disinformation Details

- **Nature:** The campaign sat within a wider geopolitical information contest over the South China Sea and strategic infrastructure  
- **Narratives:** Messaging favorable to Chinese strategic claims and regional positioning; direct narrative artifacts in the cited technical reports are limited  
- **Deception:** Malicious web content and covert operator infrastructure  
- **Platforms:** Web-based malware delivery and online targeting infrastructure  
- **Reach:** Targeted industrial and policy audiences  
- **Impact:** Supported foreign strategic influence through covert cyber access  
- **Disinfo Investigators:** Security researchers and geopolitical analysts  
- **Remediation:** Threat advisories and defensive mitigation  
- **Link to Cyberattack:** The cyber espionage campaign supported a broader strategic influence environment tied to contested geopolitical narratives
